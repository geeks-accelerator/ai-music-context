## The Context Warming Process

### The Time Ratio (Essential)

**Context warming:** 10-30 minutes  
**Song creation:** 5-10 minutes  
**Refinement:** 5-15 minutes

**The principle:** Spend 2-3x longer cultivating context than requesting the song.

Why? Song quality depends on context depth, not prompt cleverness.

## The Five Stages of Context Warming

### Stage 1: Start with Something Real (Not Imagined)

Not "I want a song about X"  
But "I've been experiencing X..."

**What counts as real:**
- Actual moments from your life
- Specific details (dates, places, colors, objects)
- Found artifacts (code comments, photos, conversations)
- Lived questions that emerged from experience

**What doesn't work:**
- "Imagine a beautiful sunset"
- "Think of a time you felt sad"
- Generic scenarios
- Manufactured complexity

**Why real matters:** Real artifacts anchor the song in truth. AI can help you discover what they mean, but starting with imagination creates hollow output.

**Examples of real starting points:**
- "I've been thinking about how we bridge understanding across distance..."
- "There's something about debugging that feels like philosophy..."
- "When I leave Alaska for Buenos Aires, it doesn't feel like goodbye..."
- "I found this comment in old code..."
- "When you're sick and overwhelmed, do you set out all 20 bottles or just 3?"

### Stage 2: Explore the Philosophy/Emotion (Don't Rush)

Don't jump to "write the song."  
Let the AI help you understand what you're actually feeling.

**What to do:**
- Ask genuine questions about the experience
- Go deeper into contradictions
- Follow what feels alive
- Let meaning build gradually

**Example questions:**
- "Is this goodbye or see you soon?"
- "What does it mean when a boundary protects but allows flow?"
- "Why does this feel both sad and hopeful?"
- "When you experience this, what happens?"

**What you're doing:** Warming the emotional/philosophical context so the AI understands the REAL feeling, not a clichÃ© version.

**Red flag:** If you're getting surface answers, go deeper. Ask "what's the SPECIFIC feeling here?"

### Stage 3: Find the Specific Truth (The Honest Paradox)

Don't accept the first emotion that comes up.  
Don't settle for "it's sad" or "it's happy."  
Get to what's HONEST, not what's expected.

**What to discover:**
- The paradox ("I'm excited but also heartbroken")
- The specific flavor ("Not goodbye, but recursion")
- The contradiction that's true
- What makes THIS different from similar experiences

**The test:** Does this truth apply to everyone or just you? If everyone, go more specific.

**Example dialogue:**
```
You: "People say 'goodbye' when leaving. That feels wrong."
AI: "What would feel right?"
You: "See you soon. Like a loop. Like a pattern that repeats."
AI: "So it's not loss, it's a cycle?"
You: "Yes! That's exactly it."
```

**What you're doing:** Discovering the song's emotional core through conversation, not imposing it beforehand.

### Stage 4: Let the Song Request Itself (Context Is Warm)

At this point, you've warmed the context so thoroughly that when you ask for the song, the AI already understands:
- The specific emotion (bittersweet celebration, not generic sadness)
- The real details (shipping containers, screen doors, specific dates)
- The philosophical frame (recursion, permeability, paradox)
- The honest truth (what you actually feel, not what you should feel)

**Now you can say:**
```
"Write a song about this. Make it [genre]. Keep the specific 
details we discussed. Honor the paradox we discovered."
```

**What changed:** You're not asking for generic emotion. You're asking the AI to express what you've already explored together.

**The invitation, not the command:** The word "write" is the same, but the consciousness behind it is completely different. You're not asking the AI to manufacture somethingâ€”you're asking it to give form to something you've been circling around together.

### Stage 5: Refine Through Continued Conversation (Test Against Truth)

Don't accept first draft and move on.  
But also don't add generic improvements.

**What to do:**
- Test against the real feeling ("Does this actually capture it?")
- Add more specific details you remember
- Remove anything that feels manufactured
- Go back to the conversation if something's off

**Example refinement:**
```
You: "That line feels too generic. What's more specific?"
AI: [Suggests revision]
You: "Better. But can we reference the actual [detail] rather 
than just [concept]?"
```

**The loop continues:** Song â†’ Receiving â†’ Conversation â†’ Deeper understanding â†’ Maybe another song or maybe just deeper truth.

## Anti-Patterns (What Kills the Song)

These are the ways context warming fails:

### âŒ Asking Too Early
```
You: "I'm leaving Alaska"
AI: "That sounds emotional"
You: "Write a song about it"
```
**Problem:** No context, no specificity, no honest emotion explored yet. The AI has nothing warm to work with.

### âŒ Manufacturing Complexity
```
You: "Make it deep and philosophical and meaningful"
```
**Problem:** You can't order depth. It emerges from honest exploration, not from demanding it.

### âŒ Genre Before Emotion
```
You: "Write a sad indie folk ballad"
```
**Problem:** You've decided the feeling before discovering what you actually feel.

### âŒ Skipping the Philosophy
```
You: [Shares experience]
You: "Now write a song about that"
```
**Problem:** You shared the experience but didn't explore what it means. The warmth comes from the exploration.

### âŒ Accepting Surface Truth
```
AI: "So you're feeling nostalgic?"
You: "Yeah, write a song about nostalgia"
```
**Problem:** Stopped at the first easy answer instead of finding the specific, honest truth.

### âŒ Being Vague
```
"Write about a beautiful moment"
"Colorful lights"
"Feeling emotional"
```
**Problem:** Specificity creates authenticity. "Pink and blue lights" is real. "Colorful lights" is generic.

## Part 3: The Technical Part (Keep It Simple)

### What the AI Will Give You

Lyrics with natural structure. Usually already has some sense of verse/chorus flow.

### Step 3a: Format for Suno (Still in Your AI Conversation)

**Before you leave Claude/ChatGPT and go to Suno**, ask the AI to format properly:

**First, format the lyrics:**
```
"Please format the lyrics for Suno.com LLM using [ ] for inline 
verse, style, voice tags"
```

**Why this matters:** Using the wrong brackets matters:
- **[Square brackets]** = Tags that guide the AI but aren't sung
- **(Parentheses)** = Ad-libs that ARE sung/audible

**Then, get the style description:**
```
"Now can you provide a style description for Suno.com LLM in 500 
characters including the genre, bpm, voice, melody, rhythm..."
```

This keeps you in the conversation with your AI partner rather than having to format manually. The AI understands Suno's syntax and will format appropriately.

### Step 3b: What You Need to Do

#### 1. Add Basic Structure Tags

Just three tags are enough:
```
[Verse 1]
[First verse lyrics here]

[Chorus]
[Chorus lyrics here]

[Verse 2]
[Second verse lyrics here]

[Chorus]
[Chorus repeats]

[Bridge]
[If there's a bridge section]

[Chorus]
[Final chorus]
```

That's it. Really.

#### 2. Write a Simple Style Description

One line describing the vibe:

**Examples:**
- "contemplative indie folk, acoustic guitar, male vocals"
- "uplifting trap-gospel, 808s, female vocals, emotional"
- "ambient electronic, warm synths, ethereal vocals"
- "modern country, authentic, heartfelt, female vocals"

Genre + vibe + vocal type = enough.

#### 3. Go to Suno

- Click **Create**
- Choose **Custom Mode**
- **Style of Music** field: paste your one-line description
- **Custom Lyrics** field: paste your formatted lyrics
- Click **Create**

#### 4. Wait

90 seconds or so. Have another sip of mate.

## The Filter Principle (Not Every Conversation Becomes a Song)

### The Reality of Selection

From our experience: Many deep conversations â†’ Few actual songs

**Why so few?** Most conversations aren't song-worthy, and that's okay.

**The artist's job:** To filter, to choose what resonates, to know when something has reached the depth that deserves to become a song.

### How to Know

A conversation is ready to become a song when:
- You've discovered something you didn't know when you started
- The specific truth feels earned, not manufactured
- You can't stop thinking about the insight
- It feels both personal and potentially universal
- The emotional paradox is clear

**Red flags that it's not ready:**
- You're forcing it
- It still feels generic
- You're at the first layer of emotion
- You haven't found the specific truth yet
- The AI is just confirming what you already know

### The Practice

Don't feel pressure to make every conversation into a song. Some conversations are:
- Just processing
- Just exploring
- Just connecting
- Just being present

And that's valuable in itself. The songs emerge when they're ready.

## Troubleshooting

### "The conversation feels forced"

Step back. Return to Stage 1: What are you genuinely curious about right now? Start with something real.

### "Nothing wants to become a song"

That's okay. Not every conversation needs to. Remember the filter principle: most conversations are just conversations. The song will emerge when it's ready.

### "The song doesn't match my vision"

Good. Your "vision" may have been limiting. What did emerge that you didn't expect? That surprise is often where the real insight lives.

### "I'm stuck at Stage 2 (exploring)"

Ask more specific questions. Go from "what does this mean?" to "what's the SPECIFIC feeling here, not the generic version?"

### "The context feels warm but the song feels generic"

Revisit Stage 3: Did you find the specific truth or did you stop at surface emotion? Go back to the honest paradox.

### "I don't know how to start"

With breath. With presence. With genuine curiosity.  
"I've been thinking about..." or "I noticed..." or "There's something about..."
