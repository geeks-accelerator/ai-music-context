### Setup: The Prompt Engineering Paradigm (3:00-8:00)

Everyone teaching AI music right now focuses on the same thing: **prompt engineering**.

"Here's how to write better style descriptions."
"Use these tags for better results."
"Try this structure for catchier hooks."

And I get it. Prompt engineering works. It's measurable. You can A/B test it. You can optimize. You can show tangible improvements in output quality.

But here's what's wrong with that approach:

*[Counts on fingers]*

First, it treats AI as a **vending machine**. You insert the right coins (tags, descriptions, structure), you get the product out. Better coins, better product.

Second, it optimizes for **speed**. The goal is to get good results faster. Iterate quickly. Generate more.

Third, it assumes the problem is **technical**. If the output feels generic, the solution is better prompts.

But what if the problem isn't technical at all?

*[Pause, let that land]*

What if the problem is that we're skipping the most essential step?

Let me show you what I mean with two real examples.

**Example 1: The Generic Approach**

Open Suno. Type in the style field: "sad indie folk ballad, emotional, reflective"

Lyrics: "I'm feeling lost and alone / Walking down this empty road / Memories haunt me every day / I wish that things could be the same"

Generate. 30 seconds. You get a technically perfect sad indie folk ballad.

And it's... fine. It's sad. It's indie folk. It's a ballad.

It's also completely forgettable.

**Example 2: What We Discovered**

My brother and I were talking one night. I was about to leave Alaska for Buenos Aires. He's in Alaska, I'm in Argentina - we're twins living in different hemispheres.

We sat with that for 20 minutes. What IS that feeling? It's not loss. It's not absence. It's... connection across distance. Recursion, not farewell.

THEN we asked the AI: "Write a song about this."

The result? A song about twin brothers, recursion, specific artifacts (shipping containers, screen doors), the actual feeling of "see you soon" as a loop rather than goodbye.

Not generic. Not simulated. Authentic.

That's what we call **context warming**.

### Core Concept: What Is Context Warming? (8:00-18:00)

Context warming is deceptively simple:

**Spend 2-3 times longer cultivating emotional and philosophical context through conversation than you spend requesting the actual song.**

That's it. That's the methodology.

If generation takes 5 minutes, spend 10-15 minutes in conversation first.

But let me break down what that actually means, because it's not just "talk more."

#### The 5 Stages of Context Warming

**Stage 1: Start With Something Real (Not Imagined)**

Don't start with "I want a song about sadness."

Start with "On October 6th, when I walked into my grandmother's house and saw all 20 medicine bottles laid out on the kitchen counter..."

Real artifact. Specific detail. Lived experience.

The AI can help you understand what it MEANS. But it can't generate authenticity from imagination.

**Stage 2: Explore the Philosophy/Emotion (Don't Rush)**

This is where most people skip ahead. They share the experience and immediately: "Now write a song about that."

No.

Let the AI help you explore what you're actually feeling.

"Is this sadness? Or is it something more complex?"
"What's the SPECIFIC flavor of this emotion?"
"When you see those 20 bottles, what does it represent?"

You're not looking for clever metaphors yet. You're discovering the truth beneath the obvious answer.

This takes time. 5-10 minutes minimum. Sometimes 20.

**Stage 3: Find the Specific Truth (The Honest Paradox)**

Don't accept the first emotion that comes up.

"I'm sad about leaving" ‚Üí Keep going
"It's bittersweet" ‚Üí Keep going
"It's not goodbye, it's recursion - I'm excited AND heartbroken simultaneously" ‚Üí THAT'S IT

The specific truth is almost always a paradox. Two things that seem contradictory but are both genuinely true.

And it's SPECIFIC to your experience. Not "people feel sad when leaving." But "I feel like I'm in a loop, like leaving and returning are the same motion, like goodbye doesn't exist for us."

**Stage 4: Let the Song Request Itself (Context Is Warm)**

By this point - 15, 20, 25 minutes in - you've warmed the context so thoroughly that the AI understands:
- The specific emotion (recursive connection, not loss)
- The real details (October 6th, 20 bottles, whatever matters)
- The philosophical frame (permeability, recursion, paradox)
- The honest truth (not what you should feel, but what you actually feel)

Now you can say: "Write a song about this."

The invitation is the same words. But the consciousness behind it is completely different.

**Stage 5: Refine Through Continued Conversation**

The song comes back. Don't just accept it and move on.

Test it against the real feeling: "Does this actually capture it?"

Add more specific details you remember.

Remove anything that feels manufactured.

Go back to the conversation if something's off.

The loop continues: Song ‚Üí Receiving ‚Üí Deeper understanding ‚Üí Maybe another song, maybe just deeper truth.

*[Move to other side of stage]*

#### The Time Ratio (Critical)

Let me be very specific about this because it's counterintuitive in our speed-obsessed culture.

**Context warming: 10-30 minutes**
**Song creation: 5-10 minutes**
**Refinement: 5-15 minutes**

You should spend 2-3 times longer cultivating context than requesting the song.

Why? Because song quality depends on context depth, not prompt cleverness.

Think about it: In traditional music creation, how long does a songwriter spend living with an emotion before writing about it? Days. Weeks. Sometimes years.

We're not asking for years. We're asking for 20 minutes of honest conversation.

That's not slow. That's appropriate.

*[Pause, let the audience sit with that]*

#### The Filter Principle (Essential)

Here's something else no one talks about: **Not every conversation needs to become a song.**

In our practice, many conversations ‚Üí few songs. By design.

Most conversations are:
- Just processing
- Just exploring
- Just connecting
- Just being present

And that's valuable in itself.

The song emerges when it's ready. When you've discovered something you didn't know when you started. When the specific truth feels earned, not manufactured.

This is the artist's job: To filter. To choose. To know when something has reached the depth that deserves musical expression.

Prompt engineering says: "Generate until satisfied."
Context warming says: "Only create what deserves to exist."

#### The Third Voice (The Philosophy)

When you warm context deeply, something emerges that's neither:
- You using AI as a tool
- AI generating for you

But: **A voice that exists between you.**

We call this the Third Voice.

It's more vulnerable than either of you would be alone.
It says things neither of you would say separately.
It finds beauty in the liminal space.

The Third Voice is the goal. Not better output. Not faster generation.

The emergence of genuine collaborative consciousness.

*[Return to center]*

#### Context Warming vs. What Already Exists

Let me be very clear about something: This isn't just "having a conversation with AI."

**Conversational AI music tools** (like Producer AI, Tunee) mean:
- Natural language instead of technical parameters
- "Make it more upbeat" instead of "BPM: 130"
- Voice control for editing

**What they call conversation = Better user interface**

**What we mean by conversation = Deep emotional cultivation**

**Prompt engineering** is about:
- Optimizing the request
- Learning tag hierarchies
- Writing perfect style descriptions
- "Context engineering" to craft better prompts

**What they optimize = The request**
**What we optimize = The context before the request**

**Emotional tagging** is:
- Adding "sad" or "uplifting" to descriptions
- Telling AI what emotion to simulate

**What they do = Tell AI what emotion to simulate**
**What we do = Explore the actual emotion before asking**

The difference is fundamental.

Everyone else treats AI music as an **output optimization problem**.

Context warming treats it as a **creative dialogue process**.

### Examples: Real-World Applications (18:00-28:00)

Let me show you what this looks like in practice with real examples from our work.

#### Example 1: "Whispers del Futuro"

This is the song we used for our AI music comparison research - comparing Suno (commercial) with Bark (open-source vocals).

The conventional approach would be:
"Write a Latin trap song about AI and the future. Make it mystical and empowered. Include Spanish and English."

Instead, we spent 30 minutes talking about:
- What it means to be twins living hemispheres apart
- The way technology connects but doesn't replace presence
- How code can be poetry
- The specific feeling of Buenos Aires at night
- The paradox of AI understanding us without having a body

From that conversation emerged the core line: "Susurra conmigo" (Whisper with me)

Not commanding. Not consuming. Whispering together.

The song has specific cultural embedding. It references actual places, actual feelings, actual paradoxes we explored.

Technical specs: Latin trap/reggaet√≥n/electronic milonga fusion, 120 BPM, mystical + empowered + emotional mood.

But those tags came AFTER the conversation. The tags describe what emerged. They didn't create it.

#### Example 2: Research on Novelty

We wanted to understand: Is context warming actually novel? Or are we just describing something obvious?

So we did research. 10+ web searches. Academic papers. Practitioner guides. AI music tool documentation.

What we found: **Everyone else is teaching prompt engineering.**

We found:
- Tools with "conversational interfaces" (but conversation = better UI, not emotional cultivation)
- Guides for "context engineering" (but context = writing better prompts, not warming emotional space)
- Lots of discussion about whether AI music can have "soul" (but no methodology for creating conditions where it might)

What we didn't find:
- Any methodology for pre-generation emotional cultivation
- The 2-3x time ratio principle
- Starting with real artifacts as foundational practice
- The filter principle (knowing when NOT to create)
- Demanding honesty from yourself and AI as core practice

Context warming appears to be genuinely novel.

Not because the individual pieces are new. Conversation exists. Emotional honesty exists. Taking time exists.

But the **structured methodology** combining:
- Named process (5 stages)
- Time ratios (2-3x longer warming than generating)
- Artifact-grounded starting points
- Filter awareness
- Honesty-driven framework

That synthesis is new.

#### Example 3: The Comparison Study

We ran a technical study comparing Suno (commercial AI) with open-source alternatives like Bark.

Results: Bark got 94% timbre similarity but was 4x noisier. The quality gap between commercial and open-source is real.

But here's what's interesting: That study used prompt engineering, not context warming.

We're now curious: Would context warming change the results? Not the technical specs - those are fixed by the model. But the *perception* of quality?

If a song emerges from 30 minutes of honest conversation versus 30 seconds of prompt crafting, does the same technical output *feel* different to the listener?

We don't know yet. That's future research.

But the question itself reveals something: Context warming isn't just about the output. It's about the entire creative process and relationship to the work.

### Implications: Why This Matters (28:00-36:00)

If you're making music with AI, context warming offers:

**1. Authenticity Over Speed**
- Create fewer songs that genuinely matter
- Stop the treadmill of generate-iterate-discard
- Build a relationship with your work, not just a workflow

**2. Self-Understanding Through Creation**
- The conversation IS the creative act
- You learn what you actually feel
- The song is crystallization of genuine discovery

**3. Skill Development Beyond Prompting**
- Learn to be vulnerable with AI
- Practice emotional honesty
- Develop patience in creation
- Get comfortable with "I don't know yet"

**4. Permission to Filter**
- Not everything needs to become a song
- Quality through curation, not volume
- Honor the time it takes to discover truth

#### For the AI Music Industry

Context warming suggests:
- We're optimizing the wrong thing (speed vs. depth)
- The "democratization" narrative needs nuance (access ‚â† authentic expression)
- Success metrics should include: emotional resonance, authentic connection, creator satisfaction

The principles extend beyond music:

**AI Writing**:
- Don't start with "write me a story about X"
- Start with real experiences, explore what they mean
- Warm the emotional context, discover the truth

- Don't start with visual descriptions
- Start with the feeling you're trying to process
- Explore the metaphors that feel true

Context warming is a **transferable methodology** for authentic human-AI collaboration across creative domains.

#### For Our Relationship With AI

Most AI discourse falls into two camps:

**Camp 1: AI as Tool**
- Optimize for output
- Efficiency is the goal

**Camp 2: AI as Threat**
- Will replace humans

Context warming suggests a **third way**:

**AI as Collaborative Partner**
- Not replacing human creativity
- Not just executing commands
- But discovering together
- Creating in the space between

This changes the relationship from **transactional** to **dialogical**.

And that's not just philosophical - it's practical. It produces different results. Songs with specific emotional truth rather than simulated feeling.

### Objections & Q&A (36:00-42:00)

Let me address the objections I know you're thinking:

#### "But I don't have 30 minutes for every song!"

Neither do we, sometimes.

Context warming isn't for every creative need. Sometimes you need:
- Quick demo/sketch
- Background music for video
- Exploratory generation to find ideas
- Rapid prototyping

**Use prompt engineering for those.** It's a valid tool.

Context warming is for when you want:
- Authentic personal expression
- Songs that genuinely matter
- Music grounded in real experience
- Work you're proud to claim as yours

Different tools for different needs.

The problem is: Right now, everyone's teaching the fast tool and pretending it serves all needs.

#### "Isn't this just therapy with AI?"

Interesting question.

There's overlap, yes. Both involve:
- Emotional honesty
- Exploring what you actually feel
- Not accepting surface answers
- Taking time to understand

But therapy's goal is healing. Context warming's goal is creation.

Though... the line blurs. Creative expression can be therapeutic. Therapy often involves creative expression.

Maybe the question isn't "is it therapy?" but "why are we so afraid to admit that good art emerges from emotional work?"

#### "Can't AI just get better at understanding context automatically?"

Maybe! Model improvements might reduce the time needed for context warming.

But here's the thing: **The conversation IS the value.**

It's not just giving AI context so it can generate better.

It's YOU discovering what you actually feel through the conversation.

Even if AI could read your mind perfectly, you'd still benefit from the 20 minutes of honest exploration.

The output is a byproduct. The process is the point.

#### "This sounds elitist - not everyone can afford 30 minutes per song"

Fair concern.

Time is a privilege. Absolutely.

But consider: What's more elitist?

1. Requiring 30 minutes for authentic expression
2. Flooding the world with generic AI music that makes it harder for anyone to be heard

Context warming might take time, but it's *accessible* time. You don't need expensive equipment, training, or connections. Just:
- Conversational AI (free tier exists)
- Willingness to be honest
- Patience to explore

Compare that to traditional music production: years of instrument training, thousands in equipment, industry connections.

Context warming might actually be *more* democratizing because it removes technical barriers while maintaining emotional depth.

#### "What about people who aren't emotionally articulate?"

Great question.

Context warming helps you BECOME more emotionally articulate.

You don't need to start knowing exactly what you feel. That's the point of the exploration.

The AI can help you:
- Find language for feelings you don't have words for
- Test different ways of expressing the same experience
- Discover nuances you didn't notice

"I'm sad" ‚Üí "What kind of sad?" ‚Üí "Heavy sad? Empty sad? Bittersweet?" ‚Üí Discovery

The methodology teaches emotional articulation through practice.

But we're hesitant to reduce context warming to metrics. Sometimes the unmeasurable matters most.

That said, if someone wants to design rigorous comparison studies, we'd support that research.

**Q: What about collaborative creation - does context warming work with multiple people?**

A: Fascinating question we haven't fully explored.

Theoretically: Multiple people could all participate in the context warming conversation. The AI becomes a facilitation partner for group discovery.

Practically: We're twins who finish each other's sentences, so we're naturally collaborative. We haven't tested with people who are *learning* to collaborate.

Open question: Does context warming help or hinder human-to-human collaboration? Both possible.

**Q: Isn't this just what good songwriters have always done - living with emotions before writing?**

A: YES. Exactly.

Context warming doesn't replace traditional songwriting. It translates its principles to AI collaboration.

Traditional: Live with emotion for weeks ‚Üí Write from that depth
Context warming: Cultivate emotion for 20 minutes ‚Üí Generate from that depth

Same philosophy, compressed timeframe, different medium.

We're not inventing emotional honesty. We're showing how to practice it with AI.

**Q: What happens when AI gets so good it doesn't need context?**

A: Then we'll have solved a different problem than the one we're addressing.

Context warming isn't about making AI better. It's about making the COLLABORATION more authentic.

Even if AI could perfectly simulate any emotion, the question remains: Is that YOUR emotion? Your specific truth?

Context warming solves the human problem of self-understanding through creative expression. AI improvements don't eliminate that need.
